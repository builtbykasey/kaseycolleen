About

I was 9 or 10 when my dad told me I couldn’t play basketball next door with the teenagers, they were too many years older than me. We didn’t have a basketball hoop so that meant I couldn’t play at all, right?

No. I just built my own. I found a 4x4 to use as the post, laid 2x4’s flat and nailed them together to make a backboard, used a handsaw to cut a 5 gallon bucket in half so the top could be used as the basket. I was even able set it to a height so I could dunk!

So, why am I telling you this?

While these days I've traded in the basketball for running shoes, I think this story perfectly captures who I am. I'm the kind of person that, at 10 years old, builds a basketball hoop when I don’t have one. I’m the kind of person that, at my first office job, gets a $3/hour raise and promotion my third day on the job. I'm the kind of person that goes from answering phones at the reception desk to Director of Operations in three years. I'm the kind of person that passed 2 securities license exams in four months when the normal time is six months, per license. I'm the kind of person that completed my Certified Financial Planner education in 11 months when the normal time for completion is two years. I'm the kind of person that is running for my first ever marathon this year as training for an IRONMAN.

I’m the kind of person that works hard but doesn't take myself to seriously, that is always willing to help out a teammate, that laughs at myself when I fall walking up the stairs, that sees the glass half full, that gets excited about learning, that doesn't hide my enthusiasm for life!
