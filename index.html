About

I was 9 or 10 when my dad told me I couldn’t play basketball next door with the teenagers, they were too many years older than me. We didn’t have a basketball hoop so that meant I couldn’t play at all, right?

No. The obvious answer to me was to just build my own. I found a 4x4 to use as the post, laid 2x4’s flat and nailed them together at an angle to make a backboard then I used a handsaw to cut a 5 gallon bucket in half so the top could e used as the basket. Finally I tied it to a tree to hold it upright, I was even able set it to a height so I could dunk!

Why am I telling you this? I think this story best sums up who I am. I respect the rules but am relentless towards my goals. I love being athletic. I constantly challenge myself to find new solutions and grow beyond my current capabilities. I play well with others but am just fine playing on my own.

How did the story end? I played on that basketball hoop constantly, eventually wearing out the grass where I ran and dribbled until my dad told me to go next door to play basketball so the grass could grow back. :)
